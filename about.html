About UniChat

UniChat is a prototype AI assistant for Carleton’s Information Resource Management (IRM) community. It is designed to help students, staff, and faculty quickly find reliable information about IRM courses, program requirements, and related resources by searching across curated university content.

Project Goals

The UniChat project explores how retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) can:

  • Make course and program information easier to find and understand.
  • Reduce repetitive questions sent to instructors and staff.
  • Provide students with a single, consistent entry point to IRM information.
  • Evaluate different LLMs and retrieval strategies in a real academic setting.

How UniChat Works

UniChat uses a RAG pipeline: your question is converted into a vector, relevant IRM documents are retrieved, and an LLM generates an answer grounded in those sources. We experiment with:

  • Multiple embedding models and vector indexes.
  • Different LLMs (e.g., Gemini, Claude, Llama 3.2, Mistral).
  • Several RAG generation methods such as baseline top-k, re-ranking, query rewriting, and self-checking.

The interface you see is intentionally simple and Carleton-branded so the focus stays on the quality of the answers.

What UniChat Is Not

UniChat is an experimental tool and not an official academic advisor or policy authority. For high-stakes questions (e.g., degree audits, appeals, or formal deadlines), always confirm details with:

  • Your official course outline on Brightspace.
  • The IRM program office or departmental websites.
  • Academic advising resources at Carleton.

Quick Facts

  • Status: Prototype / research project
  • Audience: IRM students, staff, and faculty
  • Scope: Selected IRM course outlines and program documents
  • Access: Internal testing environment (not a public service)

Project Team

UniChat is developed as a capstone project within the Bachelor of Information Technology – Information Resource Management program. The team is responsible for:

  • Designing the Carleton-aligned user interface.
  • Building the RAG pipeline and evaluation framework.
  • Monitoring privacy, accessibility, and ethical considerations.
